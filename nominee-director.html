What is a nominee director

Your company's representative on paper

A nominee director is a professional appointed to represent your company's interests in official records without being involved in day-to-day management. They appear as a board member in government and legal registers, following a pre-agreed mandate that leaves full control with you.

📋

Official Representation

🔒

Full Privacy Protection

⚖️

Regulatory Compliance

Ensures your company meets UAE Commercial Companies Law requirements without any operational interference.

🛡️

100% Control Retained

You retain full decision-making authority. The nominee cannot make business decisions without your explicit written consent.

Nominee vs Regular

Nominee Director vs Regular Director

The distinction is critical. Understanding it prevents confusion and protects your control.

✕ Regular Director

  • ✕ Actively manages daily business operations
  • ✕ Makes strategic and financial decisions
  • ✕ Holds fiduciary responsibilities to shareholders
  • ✕ Full decision-making authority
  • ✕ Personally liable for corporate obligations

✓ Nominee Director

  • ✓ Non-executive — no operational involvement
  • ✓ Bound by a strict mandate from owners
  • ✓ Compliance-focused role only
  • ✓ Ultimate control stays with beneficial owner
  • ✓ Protected by indemnity agreements

FAQ

Common questions about nominee directors

Straight answers to the questions we hear every day.

What is a nominee director in Dubai? ↓
A nominee director is a professional appointed to represent a company on paper, appearing in official government and legal records as a board member. They do not manage the business — their role is strictly compliance-focused and bound by a legal agreement with the beneficial owner.
Is a nominee director legally allowed in the UAE? ↓
Yes. Nominee director arrangements are recognised under UAE Commercial Companies Law (CCL) as long as they are properly documented with written agreements that define authority limits and comply with AML regulations.
Does a nominee director have control over my company? ↓
No. A nominee director's powers are strictly limited by a legal agreement. The beneficial owner retains full control through a Power of Attorney and the Nominee Director Agreement. The nominee cannot make business decisions or interfere in operations.
When do I need a nominee director in Dubai? ↓
You may need a nominee director if: your company structure requires a UAE-resident director, you want to keep your name off public records, you are a foreign investor without UAE residency, or you need smooth compliance for banking and government filings.
How long does the appointment process take? ↓
With Aspira, the full process — from consultation to official registration — typically takes 2–3 days. This includes agreement drafting, document preparation, and submission to the relevant authority.
How much does nominee director service cost? ↓
Costs vary based on company structure, jurisdiction, and engagement duration. Aspira offers transparent, competitive pricing with no hidden charges. Contact us for a personalised quote based on your needs.
What documents protect my ownership? ↓
Three key documents: the Nominee Director Agreement (defines scope), Power of Attorney (gives you override authority), and Indemnity Agreement (protects both parties). All are legally binding and drafted by legal professionals.
Do you offer nominee services for all jurisdictions? ↓
Yes. We provide nominee director services for free zone companies (DMCC, IFZA, RAK ICC, DKP, etc.), mainland companies (DET), and offshore structures. Each appointment is tailored to the specific jurisdiction's requirements.
